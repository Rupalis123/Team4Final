Age Risk Explanation

To better understand age in correlation with mortality risk, we applied clustering method using KMeans=6 with age and mortality columns only. For visualization, data was grouped and sorted by predicted clusters and calculated for age ranges and average mortality risk within the clusters. According to the machine learning model, cluster 0 or ages over 65 were at highest risk for Covid19 mortality. This is consistent with vaccine policy of ages 65+ being one of the first population groups to qualify for phase 1. Interesting to note was cluster 5 or ages 12 to 25 which had a higher mortality risk than older populations. This may be due to an increase in substance abuse, unmanaged mental health illnesses as seen by increase in opioid overdoses and mental health hospitalizations.
