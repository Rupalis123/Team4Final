Logistic Regression Risk Model

Our Risk Model Explanation

Our risk model initially uses the classifier method to narrow contributing factors with higher feature importance for covid19 mortality predictions which are age, gender and the top 6 features of each category in pre-existing conditions and symptoms.

Then, logistic regression model is applied which shows an accuracy score of 0.88. Although the low-risk population scores are in the 92th percentile, the high risk population scores are in the 66th percentile shows more reliability on predicting low risk rather than high risk populations. In a clinical setting, it would be preferred to identify high risk individuals so that they may receive more aggressive medical care.

The confusion matrix shows that the logistic regression model is able to correctly identify 87.5% (75% true low risk = predicted low risk + 12.5% true high risk = predicted high risk) of its test data into their respective risk category which shows a strong mortality risk classification capability.
